ADO

Ado (Japanese: アド) is a Japanese singer and songwriter. She had her debut single at age 17 with "Usseewa", which rose to number 1 on "Billboard Japan Hot 100". She since has gone on to release two studio albums "Kyōgen" and "Zanmu" and the songs for the character Uta in One Piece Film: Red. She is under the artist management company Cloud Nine. She then collabed with Sanrio and created the character Adorozatorumary.

Early life

Ado became interested in music in 2014 when the video sharing website NicoNico was released on the 3Ds. She was impressed by the Utaite singers on the platform who would not show their faces. Later in early 2017, Ado, who was fourteen then, became an utaite herself and started with a cover of the vocaloid song "Kimi no Taion".

A little later on in December 23 2019, she featured on Kujira's digital single "Kinmokusei", also known as "Osmanthus". A couple of months after that she was featured as a vocalist on the single "Shikabanēze" by Jon-Yakitory. Then, in May 2020, she participated in the compilation album Palette4 created by Pony Canyon. She recorded the songs Call boy and Taema Naku Ai iro for the Album.

Major label debut and first album

Ado made her Label debut with Virgin music which in a sublabel of Universal music Japan. On October 23, before the day of her 18th birthday, she released "Usseewa" which was her debut single and was produced by the vocaloid producer Syudou. On December 10, 2020, "Usseewa" ranked number 1 on Spotify Viral 50 Japan. Soon she released her second single "Readymade" written by Vocaloid producer Surii. On February 14 2021, she released her third single, "Gira Gira", written by Vocaloid producer teniwoha. On April 27, 2021, Ado released her fourth single "Odo", which was used in an NHK show about Vocaloid producers. On June 14, 2021, she released her fifth single, "Yoru no Pierrot". Her sixth single "Aitakute" was released on August 12.

In October, Ado announced her debut studio album, Kyōgen. Released on January 26, 2022, Kyōgen sold over 140,000 physical copies in its first week, a first for a female artist since Superfly's 2008 self titled debut album. Debuting at number one on both the Billboard Japan Hot Albums chart and the Oricon Albums chart, Ado become the first woman to have a debut album peak at number one on its first week on Japanese charts since Miwa's 2011 studio album, Guitarissimo.
